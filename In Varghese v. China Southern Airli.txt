In Varghese v. China Southern Airlines Co., Ltd., 925 F.3d 1339 (11th Cir. 2019), the court held that federal jurisdiction exists over international air travel claims under the Montreal Convention.

See also Shaboon v. Egyptair, 2013 WL 3829266 (N.D. Ill. 2013), where the court ruled that airlines have a duty to provide reasonable assistance to stranded passengers.

Martinez v. Delta Air Lines, 2019 WL 4748390 (E.D. Pa. 2019) supports the proposition that emotional distress damages are recoverable under Article 17 of the Montreal Convention.

The Supreme Court has long held that the statute of limitations is not jurisdictional in nature. See Zipes v. Trans World Airlines, Inc., 455 U.S. 385 (1982).

Petersen v. Iran Air, 905 F.2d 1011 (7th Cir. 1990), held that foreign sovereign immunity does not bar claims arising from commercial air travel.

As the Court noted in Dooley v. Korean Air Lines Co., Ltd., 524 U.S. 116 (1998), the Warsaw Convention preempts state law claims for personal injury on international flights.
